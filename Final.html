Biryani is a type of rice dish that originated in India and is now popular in many parts of South Asia and the Middle East. It is typically made with long-grain rice (such as basmati) that is cooked with spices, vegetables, and meat (such as chicken, beef, lamb, or shrimp). The ingredients are usually layered in a pot and cooked on low heat until the rice is tender and the flavors have melded together. Biryani can be served as a main course or as a side dish, and it is often accompanied by raita (a yogurt-based condiment) or other accompaniments. There are many variations of biryani, and the ingredients and spices used can vary greatly depending on the region and personal preference.
